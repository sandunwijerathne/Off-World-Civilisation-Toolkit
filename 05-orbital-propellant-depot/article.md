# The Orbital Propellant Kelp Farm

*Category: Spacecraft & Space Journeys · Off-World Civilisation Toolkit*

Orbit's first industry will be selling water. The Kelp Farm is a depot station that captures asteroid or lunar ice deliveries, electrolyses the water into hydrogen and oxygen using acres of solar arrays (the 'kelp fronds' that give it the name), liquefies the gases, and stores them in sun-shielded cryogenic tanks. Ships arriving from Earth launch nearly empty and fill their tanks in orbit — which changes the rocket equation more than any engine improvement ever could.

Every subsystem exists: electrolysis runs daily on the ISS, cryogenic fluid management is under active NASA flight demonstration, and orbital refuelling is core to at least one operational launch architecture. The novelty is integration and the supply chain — mining and delivering the ice. The depot is the anchor customer that makes asteroid mining a business rather than a dream.

**Status: arguably the most commercially inevitable machine in this catalogue.**
