# The Magsail Cruiser

*Category: Spacecraft & Space Journeys · Off-World Civilisation Toolkit*

A ship with no engine and no fuel tank. The Magsail Cruiser unfurls a superconducting loop tens of kilometres across, drives a persistent current through it, and generates an artificial magnetosphere — a magnetic scoop that deflects the charged particles of the solar wind. Every deflected proton pushes back, and the ship rides the wind like a yacht with an invisible sail. Turning the loop tacks the vessel; collapsing the field is the brake.

The physics comes from Zubrin and Andrews' magnetic sail papers of the late 1980s, and the mini-magnetosphere effect has been reproduced at laboratory scale. Thrust is gentle — millinewtons per square kilometre of effective sail — but it never runs out, making the magsail ideal for slow bulk freight and, crucially, for decelerating incoming interstellar probes without carrying braking fuel. The blocker is materials: high-temperature superconducting cable by the tonne, deployed and kept cold in vacuum.

**Status: mid-century material.**
