## Blueprint prompt

```
Technical engineering blueprint of the "Magsail Cruiser MS-1 Aeolus," a
superconducting magnetic-sail freighter spacecraft, drawn as a vintage cyanotype
schematic: deep prussian-blue background, fine white and cyan linework, faint
drafting grid. Plan view of a giant ring: a thin superconducting coil hoop two
kilometres in diameter drawn as a large circle, eight thin kevlar rigging spokes
converging on a small square central bus with cryocooler radiator fins, a small
gridded solar panel, and a cargo docking berth. Four small plasma injector nozzles
on the hoop at cardinal points. Dashed concentric ellipses showing dipole magnetic
field lines, a large dashed parabolic boundary on the left labeled "MAGNETOPAUSE,"
and streamline arrows of solar wind entering from the left and deflecting around
the bubble. Inset diagram top-right showing a tilted sail ellipse with wind arrows
and labeled lift/drag thrust vectors. Magnified detail circle of the
superconducting cable cross-section with concentric layers labeled MLI wrap,
structural jacket, cryogenic channel, REBCO tape stack. Numbered callout circles
with leader lines, dimension line reading "COIL DIAMETER 2,000 m," parts list
panel, and engineering title block reading "MS-1 AEOLUS — GENERAL ARRANGEMENT."
Monospaced drafting typography, flat orthographic projection, no shading, no
photorealism.
```
