# The Lagrange Loom

*Category: Spacecraft & Space Journeys · Off-World Civilisation Toolkit*

Every rocket throws away its road. The Lagrange Loom is a machine that builds one instead: a 12-tonne robotic weaver that manufactures ultra-strong tether ribbon in orbit and strings it into a permanent web between low Earth orbit and the Earth-Moon Lagrange points. Rotating tether segments catch payloads at their tips and fling them onward, exchanging momentum mechanically; hanging climber lines let slow cargo winch itself uphill on electricity alone. Inbound lunar cargo literally pays the momentum bill for outbound Earth cargo, so a mature network moves freight with essentially zero propellant.

The science is old and solid: Moravec formalised the rotating skyhook in 1977, Hoyt and Forward showed a full Earth-Moon tether transport system closes on paper, and kilometre-scale tethers have already flown (SEDS-1, TSS-1R, YES2). What has never existed is the weaver itself — an autonomous machine that spins, splices, inspects, and repairs tether at orbital scale. That is a robotics problem, not a physics problem.

**Status: Phase-1 version plausible within ~20 years of a funded start.** Full paper, blueprint, and image prompts in this folder.
