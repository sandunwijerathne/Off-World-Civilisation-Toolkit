# The Cryo-Volatile Harvester

*Category: Off-World Civilisation Tools · Off-World Civilisation Toolkit*

The Moon's poles hide craters where the Sun has never risen — and in that permanent -230 °C darkness, billions of tonnes of ancient ice. The Harvester is a rover-refinery built for the coldest real estate in the inner solar system: it drives down into shadowed craters on legs that work at cryogenic temperatures, thermally mines the ice with directed microwave heating, cold-traps the released vapour, and hauls it to a crater-rim plant that cracks it into drinking water, breathable oxygen, and hydrogen-oxygen rocket fuel.

NASA's LCROSS impact confirmed the ice in 2009; orbital radar has mapped the deposits since. The brutal part is engineering machines whose lubricants, batteries, and electronics survive the cold while their radiators still work — the rover is a thermal-design problem wearing a mining costume. Whoever operates the first Harvester owns the petrol station for the entire Earth-Moon economy.

**Status: near-term; precursor prospecting missions are already flying.**
