# Cryo-Volatile Harvester "Persephone" — Image Generation Prompts

## Blueprint prompt

```
Technical engineering blueprint of the "CVH-1 Persephone," an autonomous lunar
ice-mining rover for permanently shadowed craters, drawn as a vintage cyanotype
schematic: deep prussian-blue background, fine white and cyan linework, faint
drafting grid. Side elevation of a six-wheeled rover on cold-tolerant wheels
standing on a hatched "icy regolith -230 C" ground line. From front to back: an
articulated arm reaching down and forward into the ground with a heated extraction
head and a dashed vapour shroud, cyan heat-wave and vapour arrows rising from it,
and a cyan vapour-transfer line curving back to a circular cutaway cold trap inside
the body; a battery bank; a heated "warm box" avionics enclosure; a sensor and
light mast with LIDAR and dashed headlight cones for dark navigation; and an ice
hopper at the rear. Inset panel top-right showing a crater cross-section with the
sunlit rim and a small rim processing plant, a rover in the shadowed floor, and a
dashed "ice shuttle" arrow between them, captioned "mine in dark, process in sun."
Magnified detail circle showing the sublimate-and-re-freeze cycle: heated head in
regolith, vapour rising to a cold trap. Numbered callout circles with leader
lines, a dimension line reading "ROVER LENGTH 6.2 m," a parts list panel, and an
engineering title block reading "CVH-1 PERSEPHONE — GENERAL ARRANGEMENT."
Monospaced drafting typography, flat orthographic projection, no shading, no
photorealism.
```

## Most-buildable real product prompt (near-term ice-prospecting/extraction rover)

```
Photorealistic render of a near-future lunar ice-mining rover inside a permanently
shadowed polar crater, lit almost entirely by its own onboard floodlights and a
faint blue reflected earthshine: a rugged six-wheeled rover with a gold-foil and
white-panel body, a heated auger/probe arm pressed into grey icy regolith at the
front with a slight wisp of sublimating vapour, bright LED headlights casting hard
cones into pitch-black surroundings, a LIDAR sensor mast, radioisotope power unit
with fins, and frost glinting on its lower structure. The crater floor is near-
black grey regolith with sharp shadows; the crater rim in the far distance catches
a thin sliver of raw sunlight against the black sky. Fine engineering detail:
cabling, dust on wheels, a printed label "POLAR ICE ROVER 01," warning placards,
thermal blankets. Shot low and close with a wide lens, dramatic single-source
lighting from the rover's own lamps, deep black lunar sky, hyper-detailed,
aerospace documentary photography style.
```
