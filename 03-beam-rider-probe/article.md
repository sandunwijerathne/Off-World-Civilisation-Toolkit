# The Beam-Rider Interstellar Probe

*Category: Spacecraft & Space Journeys · Off-World Civilisation Toolkit*

The first starship will weigh less than a coin. A beam-rider is a gram-scale probe bonded to a metres-wide reflective lightsail, accelerated to a meaningful fraction of light speed by a phased ground-based laser array delivering gigawatts for a few minutes. At 15-20% of c, Alpha Centauri is a twenty-year cruise instead of a seventy-thousand-year one; the probe photographs the system as it flashes through and radios the images home across 4.4 light-years.

This is the least speculative entry in the catalogue: Breakthrough Starshot has funded the engineering studies since 2016, laboratory lightsails have survived relevant accelerations, and phased-array laser technology improves annually for unrelated commercial reasons. The hard problems are the sail's reflectivity (absorbing even a fraction of a percent of a gigawatt vaporises it), pointing stability on the beam, and building a transmitter that can be heard from another star.

**Status: prototype swarm launches plausible within a generation.**
