# The Hibernation Transit Pod

*Category: Spacecraft & Space Journeys · Off-World Civilisation Toolkit*

The cheapest cargo to send to Mars is a sleeping human. A crew in medically induced torpor — core temperature lowered a few degrees, metabolism suppressed roughly 50-70% — consumes a fraction of the food, water, and oxygen of a waking crew, doesn't need exercise volume or entertainment, and passes the psychological gauntlet of a nine-month transit unconscious. The pod is a climate-controlled berth with automated IV nutrition, robotic nursing arms, and continuous physiological monitoring, arranged in rings so a skeleton watch (human or AI) tends the sleepers.

The foundation is already in hospitals: therapeutic hypothermia is standard care after cardiac arrest, holding patients in controlled torpor for days. NASA has funded SpaceWorks' torpor-habitat studies precisely because the mass savings cascade — less food means less shielding means less propellant. Open questions are real: months-long torpor, muscle and bone response, and safe revival protocols.

**Status: the medicine is the frontier; the engineering is nearly routine.**
